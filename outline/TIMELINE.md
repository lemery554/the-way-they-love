## Continuity constraints

- Everyone who completes the purge eventually changes; no cure or recovery occurs.
- Mark's communication degrades, then stops. His fate stays unknown.
- School remains open through institutional drift.
- Anna makes two confirmed school-day house entries: the first investigation visit is Monday, Day 20; the second journal-theft visit is Tuesday, Day 21.
- Anna's two in-person meetings with Brooke precede the climax: a casual dog-park encounter on Day 11 and the diner meeting on Friday, Day 24.
- The narrative concludes early on Saturday, Day 25, after the Friday diner meeting flows into the overnight pursuit, return, siege, escape, engineered pickup, and journal reveal.
- The changed-police "reunion" beat is cut: no officers arrive on the escape night.
- Brooke does not become sick, but the story never defines her as immune.
- Touch does not transmit the illness, but no character knows the real route and exposure scenes must preserve that uncertainty.
