# Character Revision Plan

This plan converts the post-draft character audit into chapter-level development work. It supplements `CHARACTER_ARCS.md`; it does not replace canon.

## Brooke

### Development goals

- Make filmmaking a practiced craft and future ambition.
- Show why mediated connection feels safer than direct intimacy.
- Give her a recognizable school and social life without adding romance.
- Track guilt over filming Claire, accusing Claire, and using Sarah's love to gain entry.
- Make her choice of Anna consciously desperate rather than naive.

### Chapter placements

- **Prologue–1:** Editing decisions, source verification, channel habits, ordinary footage of family.
- **2:** Workshop matters because it is a real step toward independence and film school.
- **3:** Show her enjoying Claire before the cruel argument.
- **8–11:** Let view counts, comments, and ethical discomfort alter what she films and posts.
- **12:** Establish her marginal position at school and the appeal of Anna recognizing her work.
- **15:** Adoption disclosure becomes a deliberate but regretted editorial choice.
- **18–22:** Show the cost of exposure through harassment, search results, school recognition, and loss of privacy.
- **23–25:** Track devices and demonstrate that evidence cannot produce safe authority.
- **28:** Brooke records Anna, confronts the journal suspicion, and still chooses the least immediately dangerous option.

## Claire

### Development goals

- Preserve humor, curiosity, borrowing clothes, and ordinary irritation.
- Vary changed behavior so proximity is not her only action.
- Let the beach-memory crack remain singular and brief.
- Preserve her inability to hurt Cooper.

### Chapter placements

- **1–4:** Add ordinary jokes, competition, shared boredom, and clothing conflict.
- **7–10:** Show homework, appetite, television, sleep, and humor continuing alongside wrong closeness.
- **11–13:** Let retained memory feel emotionally real before bathroom violence becomes undeniable.
- **14:** Keep tears confused and short; no coherent warning.
- **16–21:** Give her household tasks and quiet alliances with Sarah while retaining age-specific behavior.
- **26–27:** Preserve apology, journal denial, request for a hug, and obedience to Sarah without making Claire a miniature adult.

## Sarah

### Development goals

- Make her pre-purge competence, exhaustion, love, and mistakes tangible.
- Clarify that Brooke's biological-mother curiosity wounds Sarah without making Sarah unsympathetic.
- Let understandable protection gradually become captivity.

### Chapter placements

- **1–3:** Household logistics, Mark's absence, workshop cancellation, and different expectations for each daughter.
- **4–7:** Sarah manages Claire's illness, hospital bureaucracy, and Brooke's filming conflict.
- **8–15:** She needs recovery to be real and repeatedly chooses the least frightening interpretation.
- **16:** Expand the purge and Brooke's care for her.
- **17–22:** Retained habits remain loving while control becomes systematic.
- **26:** Her door hesitation combines credible safety concern with need for voluntary return.
- **27:** Her honest inability to release Brooke culminates in a barrier-breaching act she believes restores safety.

## Mark

### Development goals

- Keep fate unknown.
- Make absence increasingly painful and suspicious.
- Preserve practical dad voice rather than turning messages into puzzle clues.

### Communication ladder

- **1:** Ordinary work text and mild joke.
- **5–6:** Broken hospital call and promise to return when possible.
- **9–10:** Delayed text and final live call with ambiguous background voices.
- **15–17:** Delayed message, missed conditional call, then voicemail.
- **18–23:** No live response; Brooke drafts or sends practical updates and receives nothing reliable.
- **27:** Safe combination memory pays off a normal household emergency conversation.

## Anna

### Development goals

- Keep every private fact traceable.
- Add imperfections without making her threatening before the reveal.
- Let patience remain behaviorally advanced but humanly interpretable.

### Chapter placements

- **12:** Mundane dog-park encounter; no private knowledge.
- **19–21:** Only traces of entries, never unseen narration.
- **24:** Coffee spill, hesitation, narrow answers, plausible resemblance, and refusal to force.
- **28:** Specific surveillance location, frustrated self-correction, practical destination, no initiated touch.
- **29:** Journal possession and folded pages provide proof; no internal explanation or eye flicker.

## Cooper

### Development goals

- Remain a dog, not a supernatural detector with perfect outputs.
- Mix meaningful warnings with ordinary distraction, fatigue, appetite, and uncertainty.

### Chapter placements

- **1–4:** Ordinary begging, sleeping, and household attachment.
- **7–10:** Avoidance of Claire varies by distance and her behavior.
- **12:** Tolerates still, non-advancing Anna while remaining watchful.
- **19–21:** Entry traces are ambiguous and could resemble ordinary agitation.
- **25–29:** Injury, exhaustion, reliance on Brooke, tolerance of Anna, and final recognition of the journal.

## Protected emotional payoffs

- Brooke's cruel biological-family line to Claire.
- Claire's beach-memory tears.
- Brooke's apology for searching Claire's room.
- Brooke telling Sarah, truthfully, that Sarah is her mother and she loves her.
- Sarah saying she cannot let Brooke leave because she loves her.
- Brooke initiating the hug with Anna.
- Anna continuing to study Brooke through the journal.
