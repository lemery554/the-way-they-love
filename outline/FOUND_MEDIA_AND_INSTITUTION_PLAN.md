# Found Media and Institutional Development Plan

## Goal

Bring found media toward 10 to 15 percent of the revised manuscript while keeping normal commercial prose dominant. Every insert must be created, received, viewed, or reviewed by Brooke.

## Current diagnosis

Found media appears frequently but usually as a short message, headline, or comment. The distribution is healthy; the word share is likely below target. Expansion should deepen selected artifacts rather than placing a token insert in every chapter.

## Formatting rules

- Use blockquotes for alerts, notices, short posts, and selected comments.
- Use transcript formatting only when exact timing or wording matters.
- Keep long comment threads selective and readable.
- Preserve Brooke's prose reaction before and after each insert.
- Do not show private institutional material Brooke could not access.
- Do not let found media explain the origin or rules.

## Institutional progression

### Hospital

Move from overwhelmed treatment and uncertain isolation toward discharge language emphasizing family support. Keep staff competent, exhausted, and gradually redirected rather than suddenly sinister.

### School

Show attendance pressure, counselor reassurance, physical-comfort encouragement, delayed dismissal, changed food sharing, and adults repeating news language.

### Police and 911

Early officers perform ordinary safety work. Later policy and changed judgment recategorize separation as the problem. Brooke's missing-minor return explains identification; reduced staffing and a secured weapon explain the delayed priority before Sarah retrieves it.

### News

Use short repeated comparisons across broadcasts:

- graphic uncertainty
- recovery optimism
- softened terminology
- emotional-support guidance
- synchronized calm
- persuasion

### Internet

Keep services operational. Shift default rankings, recommendations, moderation language, and missing-person amplification. Warnings remain discoverable through deliberate effort, saved copies, and direct links.

## Measurement method for the rewrite

After each developmental batch:

1. Count total batch words.
2. Count words inside blockquotes, transcript blocks, alerts, messages, comments, and explicitly formatted media.
3. Record the percentage in the batch issue.
4. Aim for 8 to 12 percent in early batches and 10 to 15 percent across the completed manuscript.
5. Reduce inserts if they interrupt emotional scenes or make the book resemble a feed.
